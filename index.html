Sales Playbook

Plays, discovery questions, competitive intel, and templates aligned to Powerland's 12 pillars.

Managed IT Services

The anchor deal. NOC/SOC, helpdesk, remote monitoring, MDR/EDR/XDR. Recurring MRR that compounds. Target orgs with 50-500 seats who have a small internal IT team drowning in tickets.

Open: "How many people are on your IT team vs how many endpoints you support? Most orgs we talk to are running at a 1:150+ ratio — that's where things fall through the cracks. We augment, not replace."

Microsoft CSP / Cloud Licensing

Become their M365 partner of record. Free license audit finds savings, then you own the renewal. E3→E5 upsells, Copilot add-ons, Azure consumption. Sticky annual recurring revenue.

Open: "Who manages your Microsoft licensing today? We're finding most SK orgs are either overpaying or under-licensed. 30-minute audit, no cost — we usually find 15-20% savings."

Hardware Refresh — Infrastructure

Dell, HPE, Lenovo servers, storage, compute. 3-5 year refresh cycles are predictable revenue. Time outreach to budget cycles. Government = April, Schools = September, CUs = January.

Open: "When was your last server refresh? If your infrastructure is 4+ years old, you're likely spending more on maintenance than a new solution would cost — and you're at higher risk of downtime."

Security Stack

Darktrace, Netskope, Fortinet, Palo Alto, Arctic Wolf. Lead with a free security posture assessment. Every board and exec team is worried about ransomware. Fear sells. Insurance companies are mandating better security.

Open: "Has your cyber insurance provider increased your premiums or added requirements this year? We do a free security posture review — most orgs we assess in SK have 3-5 critical gaps."

Backup & Disaster Recovery

Veeam, Zerto, Backupify. Ask when they last tested a restore. Most haven't. DR plans that haven't been tested are just documents. Compliance requirements (especially health, finance, government) are your leverage.

Open: "If ransomware hit tonight — and it's not if, it's when — what's your recovery time? We can get you to a 15-minute RPO and 1-hour RTO. Can I show you how?"

Network Infrastructure

HPE Aruba, Cisco, Extreme, Juniper. Switching, wireless, SD-WAN. Schools and hospitals are big wireless buyers. Wi-Fi 6E/7 refresh cycles are happening now. Campus-wide deployments are $50K-$300K.

Open: "How old is your wireless infrastructure? With remote work and BYOD, most orgs are running 3x the devices they had 5 years ago on the same network. We do free wireless assessments."

Endpoint / Hardware — Desktops & Laptops

Dell, HP, Lenovo. Volume endpoint deals for school divisions (1:1 programs), government fleets, enterprise refreshes. Pair with Managed IT for deployment, imaging, and lifecycle management.

Open: "How are you handling your next device refresh? We can bundle procurement, imaging, deployment, and lifecycle management into a single per-device cost — no surprises."

Print & Document Management (Bridge to IT)

Your Xerox heritage opens the door. MPS, M-Files ECM, document workflows. But the real play is bridging to the full IT stack. Every print conversation should map the network and security posture.

Bridge: "While we're looking at your print fleet, I noticed you're running some older switches and your firewall is end-of-life. Would it be helpful if our team did a quick network and security review while we're here?"

AI & Copilot Workshops

Microsoft Copilot is the hottest conversation in every boardroom. Offer free 2-hour workshops. Requires E5 or Copilot add-on licenses — ties directly to CSP revenue. Low commitment, high perceived value.

Open: "Every exec I talk to wants AI but nobody knows where to start. We run a 2-hour Copilot readiness workshop — your team walks away with a real adoption plan. No cost."

RFP Response Strategy

Government and education buy through RFPs. Get on vendor lists early. Build relationships BEFORE the RFP drops — by the time it's public, the winner is usually already chosen. Use assessments to shape the spec.

Strategy: Offer free assessments 6-12 months before expected RFP. Your findings become the basis for the spec. If you shape the requirements, you win the deal. Track contract expiry dates religiously.

Software Renewals & True-ups

Every org has software renewals hitting every month. Microsoft EA/CSP, VMware, Veeam, Fortinet, etc. Become the single point of contact for all renewals. Once you own the renewal, you own the relationship.

Open: "How many different vendors are you managing renewals with? We consolidate everything — licensing, support, renewals — into one relationship. One invoice, one throat to choke, better pricing."

Virtualization & Cloud Migration

Azure Local, VMware, Nutanix. The VMware→Broadcom pricing change is driving migrations. Orgs on VMware are looking for alternatives NOW. Azure Local and Nutanix are hot alternatives.

Open: "Have your VMware costs gone up since the Broadcom acquisition? We're helping a lot of SK orgs evaluate Azure Local and Nutanix — we can run a side-by-side cost analysis for free."

Competitive intelligence for the main SK competitors. Use to position against and counter.

WBM Technologies

Their Strengths

  • Strong SK presence, offices in Regina & Saskatoon
  • Long-standing government relationships
  • Full IT services stack (managed, cloud, hardware)
  • Dell & HP partner
  • Good reputation in education

Where We Win

  • We have Xerox print relationship as door opener they don't
  • Broader vendor portfolio (Darktrace, Arctic Wolf, Nutanix)
  • Powerland national scale = better pricing on Dell/HPE/Lenovo
  • Microsoft CSP competency — aggressive M365 pricing
  • AI/Copilot workshops — they lack this capability
Counter play:

Don't compete head-to-head on what WBM does well. Anchor on security (Darktrace/Arctic Wolf), Copilot workshops, and M365 licensing savings. Be the "fresh perspective" — many accounts are fatigued with WBM.

Horizon Computer Solutions

Their Strengths

  • Established SK IT provider with strong local roots
  • Good relationships in mid-market and government
  • Managed IT and cloud services offering
  • Responsive local support team

Where We Win

  • Powerland national scale = deeper vendor pricing (Dell/HPE/Lenovo)
  • Broader security portfolio (Darktrace, Arctic Wolf)
  • Microsoft CSP competency — aggressive M365 licensing
  • AI/Copilot workshops they can't match
  • Xerox print relationship as additional door opener
Counter play:

Horizon is solid but lacks the vendor depth and national pricing we bring. Lead with security stack (Darktrace/Arctic Wolf) and M365 savings — two areas where our scale creates real dollar gaps. Position Copilot workshops as the forward-looking differentiator.

PC Place

Their Strengths

  • Well-known retail + business IT in Saskatchewan
  • Strong hardware resale and device deployment
  • Quick-turn local service for SMB
  • Trusted by smaller orgs for break/fix

Where We Win

  • Limited managed services depth — mostly hardware-focused
  • No enterprise security stack (no SOC, no SIEM)
  • Can't match our cloud and GreenLake as-a-Service model
  • No M365 CSP competency at scale
  • We offer full lifecycle — they sell boxes
Counter play:

PC Place wins on quick hardware sales to SMB. Position against them by leading with managed services and security — show the account they're buying hardware but have no monitoring, no backup strategy, no security layer. "Who's watching your network at 2 AM?"

Wolf Strata

Their Strengths

  • Focused managed IT and cloud consultancy
  • Good technical depth in Azure/M365 environments
  • Consultative approach resonates with mid-market
  • Growing reputation in SK IT community

Where We Win

  • Smaller operation — can't match Powerland's bench depth
  • Limited hardware procurement scale
  • Narrower vendor portfolio — we bring Darktrace, Arctic Wolf, Nutanix, HPE
  • No print relationship to cross-sell from
  • We offer GreenLake as-a-Service — they can't
Counter play:

Wolf Strata competes on consultative cloud/M365 — don't fight them there alone. Bundle hardware + security + managed services to show total value. Our edge is the full stack: "one partner for everything" vs their niche play.

Compugen

Their Strengths

  • National scale — large Canadian IT solutions provider
  • Strong enterprise and government contracts
  • Deep vendor partnerships (HP, Cisco, Microsoft)
  • Professional services and deployment capability

Where We Win

  • Compugen is national — we have boots on the ground in SK
  • Local relationships and faster response times
  • More flexible and agile for mid-market deals
  • Xerox print footprint gives us a warm intro they don't have
  • AI/Copilot workshops — we're proactive, they're reactive
Counter play:

Compugen wins big enterprise deals on scale. Beat them by being local and fast — "we're in Regina, not Toronto." Mid-market accounts want a partner who picks up the phone, not a ticket queue. Leverage existing Xerox relationships they can't touch.

Internal IT / "We Handle It Ourselves"

Their Argument

  • "We know our environment best"
  • "We don't want to lose control"
  • "We've always done it this way"
  • Cost perception — think outsourcing is more expensive

Where We Win

  • Augment, don't replace — we make their team better
  • 24/7 SOC they can't afford to staff
  • Better vendor pricing through Powerland volume
  • Skills gap — one IT person can't know security + cloud + networking
  • Risk reduction — what happens when their IT person quits?
Counter play:

"We augment your team, not replace them. Your IT person becomes the hero who brought in enterprise-grade security and 24/7 monitoring. And if they ever leave, you're not dead in the water."
